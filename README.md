# TASK 3 - Causality Assessment of Drug Reactions

## Objective

Evaluate the relationship between a suspected drug and an adverse drug reaction (ADR) using standard causality assessment principles.

---

## Case Summary

**Patient:** 45-year-old Female

**Suspected Drug:** Amoxicillin

**Adverse Reaction:** Skin Rash

**Reaction Onset:** 3 days after starting the medication

**Outcome:** Recovered after discontinuation of the drug

---

## Assessment Criteria

### 1. Temporal Relationship
The adverse reaction occurred after initiation of Amoxicillin, indicating an appropriate temporal association.

### 2. Dechallenge
The skin rash improved after stopping the suspected medication, supporting a causal relationship.

### 3. Alternative Causes
No stronger alternative explanation for the reaction was identified.

### 4. Rechallenge
No rechallenge was performed; therefore, this criterion could not be assessed.

---

## Causality Assessment

**Classification:** Probable

### Reason

The reaction developed within a reasonable time after starting Amoxicillin and resolved after discontinuation of the drug. The absence of a rechallenge prevents classification as certain, but the available evidence strongly supports a probable causal relationship.

---

## Learning Outcome

This exercise demonstrates the importance of:

- Evaluating temporal relationships
- Assessing dechallenge and rechallenge information
- Considering alternative causes
- Classifying ADRs using standard causality assessment methods

These principles are essential for effective pharmacovigilance and patient safety.
